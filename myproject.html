Our Facilities

Kenyatta University provides a range of facilities, including a modern post-modern library, a teaching hospital (KUTRRH), a conference center, student accommodation, and sports facilities like swimming pools and sports fields. Other notable amenities are the Kenyatta University Funeral Home, Bookshop, Leadership and Adventure Park (KUALEP), and a Business and Student Service Center

Ku Post-modern Library

The Postmodern Library (PML) is the main academic library at Kenyatta University in Kenya, housed in a modern, five-story building equipped with advanced information resources, extensive digital services, and wireless internet access. It serves over 60,000 students, offering a large collection of over a million information materials and providing advanced services like remote access to digital resources and inclusive assistive technologies for visually impaired users. The library emphasizes modern Web 2.0 possibilities and uses social media for communication, with its resources also available to government and non-governmental organizations

Ku Refferal Hospital

The KU Referral Hospital, officially the Kenyatta University Teaching, Referral & Research Hospital (KUTRRH), is a Level 6 public national referral hospital in Nairobi, Kenya, offering complex, specialized healthcare services to patients from across the country and region. It also serves as a center for medical education and research, utilizes state-of-the-art equipment and technology, and focuses on patient-centered, evidence-based care. KUTRRH provides specialized care in areas like cancer, renal, and orthopedic services, and aims to improve healthcare outcomes through its teaching and research roles

Business and Student Service Centre (BSSC)

The Kenyatta University (KU) Business and Student Service Center (BSC) is a versatile facility that combines a student service hub with a business-oriented environment. It offers students a conducive space for quiet study, meetings, and entertainment, differentiating itself from the crowded shopping areas on campus. The BSC is distinct from the Directorate of Student Affairs, which provides welfare services, and the School of Business, which offers academic programs in business-related fields.

About Us

KU is short for Kenyatta University, a leading public university in Kenya with its main campus on over 1,000 acres, offering a wide range of high-quality academic programs, a focus on practical skills, and strong ties to industry to ensure graduates are globally competitive. Established in 1985, KU provides a nurturing environment with world-class facilities, including libraries, labs, and the Chandaria Business Innovation and Incubation Centre, aimed at fostering human resource development and contributing to societal advancement through research and knowledge. Key Aspects of Kenyatta University: Leading Public University: KU is a highly regarded institution in Kenya, recognized for the quality of its graduates. Academic Excellence: The university offers diverse, high-quality academic programs designed to provide globally competitive skills and hands-on training. State-of-the-Art Facilities: KU boasts a world-class library, well-equipped laboratories, and the Chandaria Business Innovation and Incubation Centre, which supports young entrepreneurs. Focus on Practical Experience: The university emphasizes practical, hands-on knowledge and skills, and collaborates with industries to integrate professional requirements into its programs. Community Engagement and Research: KU is committed to community engagement and leverages research to improve the quality of life in society and drive new ideas. Internationalization: Through various programs, KU fosters international collaboration and student exchange, enriching its students' academic and professional experiences. Historical Overview: Origins: The university's history dates back to 1965 when the British government handed over Templer Barracks to the Kenyan government. College Status: In 1970, it became Kenyatta College, a constituent college of the University of Nairobi, and was renamed Kenyatta University College. University Status: Kenyatta University College officially became a full-fledged university on August 23, 1985.
